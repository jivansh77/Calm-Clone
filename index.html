MEDITATION & MINDFULNESS

What is meditation?

Meditation is the practice of allowing thoughts to come and go, as you learn to recognize and release them without judgment. Studies show that a long-term meditation practice can actually help shift your nervous system out of fight or flight and into the relaxed parasympathetic mode producing a wide array of benefits including:

  • Decreased anxiety and depression symptoms
  • Chronic pain management
  • Lower stress levels
  • Improved sleep quality
What is mindfulness?

Mindfulness is a practice that involves intentionally bringing one's attention and awareness to the present moment without judgment. It's about being fully present and engaged in the here and now, rather than dwelling on the past or worrying about the future. Mindfulness encourages a non-judgmental attitude towards one's thoughts, emotions and sensations, allowing them to arise and pass without any added stress. By cultivating mindfulness, people often develop a greater sense of clarity, calmness and overall wellbeing, as well as enhance their ability to respond skillfully to the challenges of life.

How do different sound frequencies affect your brain?

Sound frequency is defined by the number of wavelengths that pass a fixed point in one second, which is measured in hertz (Hz). When we listen to certain sound frequencies, it can trigger a specific neurological response in our brain. For example, low-frequency sounds like binaural beats may affect us in different ways than high-frequency sounds like a whistle.

Different frequencies can affect not only our state of alertness or relaxation, but also our mood and mental state. Some say specific frequencies can even affect healing processes in the body, although more clinical research is needed in this area.

How do binaural beats help improve focus and reduce anxiety?

Binaural beats are created by playing two different tones at slightly different frequencies in each ear, which the brain perceives as a single beat at the frequency difference between the two tones. For example, if a 200 Hz tone is played in your left ear and a 210 Hz tone in your right ear, your brain perceives a 10 Hz beat.

The theory behind binaural beats is based on brainwave entrainment — the idea that your brainwaves can synchronize with external stimuli like sound. Different frequencies are associated with different mental states:

  • Delta waves (0.5-4 Hz): Deep sleep
  • Theta waves (4-8 Hz): Relaxation, meditation, creativity
  • Alpha waves (8-13 Hz): Relaxed alertness, calmness
  • Beta waves (13-30 Hz): Concentration, alertness
  • Gamma waves (30-100 Hz): Peak concentration, cognitive enhancement

However, while some research has shown potential benefits of binaural beats for certain conditions, such as anxiety and depression, the evidence is not yet conclusive.
